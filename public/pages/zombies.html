Drifters

Profile

Estimated Age Between 20s-30s (Pre-Outbreak)
Host Origin Unknown
Infection Source Bite Transmission
Threat Level Low
Behavior Wanders in groups
Weakness Physical Damage

OVERWIEW

"Can't believe those were once humans"

Drifters are the earliest and most widespread form of infected, created through direct bite transmission. Once infected, hosts deteriorate rapidly, both mentally and physically, until little remains but primal aggression and decaying muscle memory.

Their senses are dulled, but not absent. Being in sight of one can provoke a surge of aggression. While typically slow and disoriented, Drifters become dangerous in groups, often overwhelming targets through sheer volume and relentlessness.

Field units are advised to dispatch on sight. Even a single bite is fatal — turning a healthy human into a carrier in under 90 seconds. Treat all contact as high-risk.

Sentinels

Profile

Estimated Age Betwenn 30s–40s (Pre-Outbreak)
Host Origin Law Enforcement
Infection Source Bite Transmission
Threat Level Moderate
Behavior Aggressive, Territorial
Weakness Piercing Bullets

OVERVIEW

"They used to serve and protect. Now they just kill."

Sentinels are infected former law enforcement officers, identified by remnants of tactical gear and uniforms. Unlike Drifters, Sentinels retain muscle coordination, limited tactical memory, and a strong predatory focus on perceived threats.

Their ballistic vests and helmets make them far more resilient to conventional firepower. Some even instinctively navigate terrain or patrol areas they were once assigned to.

Field units are advised to engage from a distance. Close engagement is strongly discouraged unless there is no other option. Sentinels are fewer in number but vastly more dangerous.

Strays

Profile

Estimated Age Between 20s–30s (Pre-Outbreak)
Host Origin Military Personnel
Infection Source Bite Transmission
Threat Level Moderate
Behavior Aggressive, Coordinated
Weakness Piercing Bullets

OVERVIEW

"We used to fight along, now we fight to kill them."

Strays are infected former soldiers highly dangerous remnants of military units caught during the first wave.

While most infected stagger blindly, Strays often call for their allies to take on you together. Their muscle memory and gear make them fast, relentless, and difficult to bring down with standard rounds.

Use armor-piercing ammunition and keep your distance. Strays may not be able to use guns anymore, but they still move like killers. Engagement is classified as high-risk, especially in enclosed environments.

Corroders

Profile

Estimated Age Unknown (Mutated)
Host Origin Engineered Drifter
Infection Source Bite + Mutagen Exposure
Threat Level High
Behavior Ranged, Unpredictable
Weakness Explosives / Heavy Fire

OVERVIEW

"They're experimenting on these things?"

Corroders are among the first successful attempts to mutate baseline Drifters into ranged bioweapons. Their bodies have been chemically altered to generate and expel volatile acid capable of melting flesh.

Heavily muscled and unnervingly fast, Corroders close distance quickly but prefer to hang back and bombard targets from mid-range. Their spittle is accurate, corrosive, and extremely lethal — avoid line of sight at all costs.

Due to increased resilience and erratic behavior, standard rounds are inefficient. Field units are advised to use explosives or concentrated fire. Do not approach alone.

Maulers

Profile

Estimated Age Unknown (Mutated)
Host Origin Engineered Drifter
Infection Source Bite + Steroid Mutagen
Threat Level High
Behavior Hyper-Aggressive, Melee Focused
Weakness Fire

OVERVIEW

"You can't outrun it. You outgun it"

Maulers are the result of mutagenic muscle enhancement experiments on base Drifters. Engineered for close-quarters domination, they display overwhelming strength and thickened skin. Their clawed hands and elongated limbs allow them to dismember targets in seconds.

Unlike Corroders, Maulers don’t rely on range. They charge with erratic bursts of speed and smash through obstacles. Standard barriers offer minimal resistance.

Field units are advised to deploy incendiary rounds or flame-based weapons. Their tissues, while dense, react violently to heat — fire is one of the few methods that can ensure permanent neutralization.
